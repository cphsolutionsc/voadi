Ireland's African diaspora community has grown dramatically over the past two decades. Today, more than 100,000 people of African origin call Ireland home — and a significant proportion of them are citizens or long-term residents eligible to shape the country's political future.

Yet representation in elected bodies remains strikingly low. As of 2025, fewer than five councillors of African descent hold elected office across all 31 local authorities. At national level, the gap is even more pronounced.

## Why This Moment Matters

The 2026 local and European elections present a genuine opportunity. Several constituencies — particularly in Dublin South-Central, Cork City, and Limerick — now have African-origin populations large enough to influence outcomes in tightly contested races.

What has historically been missing is not numbers, but organisation.

## What VOADI Is Doing

VOADI (Voices of Africans Diaspora Ireland) launched a voter registration drive in January 2026 targeting community centres, churches, and cultural events across eight counties. The results have exceeded expectations: more than 3,200 new registrations have been processed in partnership with local county registrars.

Beyond registration, VOADI is running a candidate mentorship programme — pairing prospective candidates with experienced community organisers, communications coaches, and elected officials who understand the practical realities of Irish politics.

## The Broader Goal

Political representation is not an end in itself. It is a mechanism for addressing the systemic inequities that African-origin residents continue to face in housing, employment, and healthcare.

A councillor or TD who understands the lived experience of the community is more likely to ask the right questions when budgets are set, when planning permissions are granted, and when social services are designed.

VOADI's work is premised on a simple belief: that communities which participate in democracy get better outcomes from it. This election cycle is the beginning of something larger.
